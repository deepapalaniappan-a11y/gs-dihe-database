# Informed Consent: GS-DIHE Institutional Questionnaire

## Purpose of the Research

The GS-DIHE Collective (Global South Collective for Disability Inclusion in Higher Education) is building an open-access database documenting disability inclusion policies, practices, and support mechanisms in higher education institutions across the Global South. This questionnaire collects information about your institution's approach to supporting students with disabilities.

## How Your Data Will Be Used

- **Institutional data** (policies, offices, accommodations, infrastructure) will be published in the open-access GS-DIHE Database under a CC BY 4.0 license. This means it will be publicly available for researchers, practitioners, and policy-makers to use with attribution.
- **Research findings** will be presented at the AHEAD 2026 conference and may be published in academic papers.
- **Your institution** will be identified by name in the database — this is a directory, not an anonymous survey.
- **Your personal details** (name, email, role) will NOT be published in the database without your explicit consent. They are collected only for follow-up and verification purposes.

## Your Rights

- **Voluntary participation**: You are free to decline participation or withdraw at any time without consequence.
- **Partial completion**: You may answer only the questions you are comfortable with. Partial responses are welcome and useful.
- **Review before publication**: Upon request, we will share your institution's entry with you for review before it is published in the database.
- **Correction**: You may request corrections to your institution's entry at any time by contacting us or opening an issue on the GitHub repository.
- **Withdrawal**: You may request that your institution's entry be removed from the database at any time.

## Data Protection

- Questionnaire responses are stored securely and accessible only to the GS-DIHE research team.
- Personal respondent details are not shared with third parties.
- The database does not contain personal information about individual students.
