# Scribe Training Curriculum: 2-Hour Workshop Plan

## Workshop Overview

**Duration:** 2 hours (120 minutes)
**Participants:** 15-20 volunteer students
**Format:** Interactive workshop with practice exercises
**Materials:** Practice exam papers, audio clips, evaluation forms

## Learning Objectives

By completion of this training, volunteers will be able to:

1. **Demonstrate verbatim transcription** of student responses without interpretation or correction
2. **Identify and avoid prohibited coaching behaviours** that compromise academic integrity
3. **Apply confidentiality protocols** for examination content and student information
4. **Transcribe technical terminology** accurately in STEM and other specialized subjects
5. **Manage examination logistics** professionally and efficiently

## Session 1: Foundations and Principles (45 minutes)

### Introduction and Context (10 minutes)
- **Disability rights framework:** Students with disabilities have equal right to demonstrate knowledge
- **Scribe role definition:** You are a transcription tool, not a tutor or academic assistant
- **Programme overview:** How scribe services fit into broader institutional accessibility

### Core Principles and Boundaries (20 minutes)

#### What Scribes DO:
- Write exactly what the student dictates, word for word
- Ask for spelling clarification of proper names or technical terms
- Read back written responses when requested by student
- Maintain professional demeanor and focus throughout examination
- Follow examination administrator instructions regarding timing and procedures

#### What Scribes NEVER DO:
- **Never prompt or hint** at answers through questions, suggestions, or body language
- **Never correct grammar, spelling, or factual errors** in student responses
- **Never engage in academic discussions** about examination content
- **Never provide opinions** about answer quality or examination difficulty
- **Never discuss examination content** with anyone during or after the examination

#### Academic Integrity Scenarios (Interactive Exercise)

**Scenario 1:** Student says "The capital of... um... what's that country again?"
- **Wrong response:** "Do you mean France? The capital is Paris."
- **Correct response:** "Please tell me what you would like me to write."

**Scenario 2:** Student dictates "The answer is 25" for a math problem, but you can see it should be 52.
- **Wrong response:** Correcting the number or looking confused
- **Correct response:** Write exactly "25" as dictated

**Scenario 3:** Student asks "Does this answer make sense to you?"
- **Wrong response:** "Well, you might want to reconsider the second part..."
- **Correct response:** "I can only write what you dictate. What would you like me to write?"

### Confidentiality and Professional Ethics (15 minutes)

#### Information Security Requirements:
- **Examination content** is confidential and must never be discussed outside the exam room
- **Student performance** information is private and must never be shared
- **Personal information** learned about students must be kept confidential
- **Social media policies:** Never photograph, share, or discuss examination experiences online

#### Professional Behavior Standards:
- **Punctuality:** Arrive 15 minutes early and stay until examination completion
- **Appearance:** Dress appropriately for formal academic setting
- **Communication:** Speak clearly and maintain professional tone
- **Reliability:** Confirm attendance 48 hours in advance; arrange replacement if unavailable

## Break (10 minutes)

## Session 2: Technical Skills and Practice (50 minutes)

### Transcription Techniques (20 minutes)

#### Verbatim Writing Standards:
- **Write exactly what is said** including incomplete sentences, corrections, and repetitions
- **Use student's exact words** even if grammar is incorrect or phrasing is awkward
- **Include self-corrections** when students change their answers
- **Maintain student's organization** of ideas and paragraph structure

#### Spelling and Clarification Protocols:
- **Ask for spelling** of proper names, technical terms, or unfamiliar words
- **Acceptable questions:** "How do you spell [word]?" or "Could you repeat that term?"
- **Unacceptable questions:** "Do you mean [alternative word]?" or "Are you sure about that spelling?"

#### Technical Terminology Practice:

**Mathematics Example:**
- Student: "The derivative of x squared plus 3x minus 7 equals 2x plus 3"
- Scribe writes: "The derivative of x² + 3x - 7 = 2x + 3"
- Practice with: calculus terms, statistical formulas, geometric descriptions

**Science Example:**
- Student: "Mitochondria are the powerhouse of the cell, containing cristae and producing ATP"
- Scribe writes exactly as dictated, asking for spelling of "cristae" if uncertain
- Practice with: chemistry equations, biology processes, physics concepts

**Engineering Example:**
- Student: "The stress-strain relationship follows Hooke's Law until the yield point"
- Practice with: circuit diagrams, material properties, structural analysis

### STEM-Specific Challenges (15 minutes)

#### Mathematical Expression Transcription:
- **Fractions:** "x over y" becomes x/y or use proper fraction notation
- **Exponents:** "x to the power of 3" becomes x³
- **Square roots:** "square root of 16" becomes √16
- **Equations:** Write mathematical symbols clearly and accurately

#### Diagram Description Assistance:
- **Students describe what they want drawn:** Write their descriptions verbatim
- **Students reference diagrams:** Help locate specific parts when asked
- **Graph interpretation:** Write student's explanation of data or trends
- **Never interpret or explain** visual information independently

#### Laboratory and Technical Writing:
- **Procedure descriptions:** Follow student's step-by-step dictation exactly
- **Data recording:** Write numbers, units, and observations as dictated
- **Conclusion statements:** Record student's analysis without adding interpretation

### Practice Session (15 minutes)

#### Exercise 1: Basic Transcription (5 minutes)
Volunteers work in pairs. One person dictates a short essay response while the other transcribes. Switch roles and compare accuracy.

**Sample prompt:** "Explain the significance of the Green Revolution in Indian agriculture."

#### Exercise 2: Technical Content (5 minutes)
Practice transcribing mathematics and science responses using provided sample problems.

**Sample problems:**
- Calculus: Find the integral of 2x + 3
- Chemistry: Balance the equation H₂ + O₂ → H₂O
- Physics: Calculate velocity using v = u + at

#### Exercise 3: Boundary Scenarios (5 minutes)
Role-play challenging situations where students ask inappropriate questions or seem to expect coaching assistance.

## Session 3: Practical Implementation (15 minutes)

### Examination Day Logistics (10 minutes)

#### Pre-Examination Preparation:
- **Arrival time:** 15 minutes before examination start
- **Materials check:** Bring extra pens, paper if required, and water
- **Student introduction:** Brief greeting and confirmation of arrangement
- **Seating arrangement:** Position for comfortable writing and student communication

#### During Examination Protocol:
- **Time management:** Help student track time remaining when requested
- **Break procedures:** Escort student during bathroom breaks if required
- **Question clarification:** Refer all academic questions to examination supervisor
- **Emergency procedures:** Know how to contact programme coordinator

#### Post-Examination Procedures:
- **Material handling:** Submit completed papers to appropriate administrator
- **Feedback completion:** Complete volunteer feedback form within 24 hours
- **Student confidentiality:** Maintain privacy about performance and content
- **Coordinator reporting:** Report any problems or concerns to programme coordinator

### Quality Assurance and Feedback (5 minutes)

#### Self-Assessment Questions:
- Did I transcribe student responses exactly as spoken?
- Did I avoid any coaching or prompting behaviors?
- Did I maintain appropriate professional boundaries?
- Would I be comfortable having this examination reviewed by administrators?

#### Continuous Improvement:
- **Regular feedback:** Complete post-examination evaluation after each session
- **Skills development:** Attend advanced training workshops when offered
- **Peer learning:** Share experiences and challenges with other volunteers
- **Programme improvement:** Suggest improvements to training and coordination

## Assessment and Certification

### Practical Competency Assessment (10 minutes)
- **Transcription accuracy test:** Write a 3-minute dictated response with 95%+ accuracy
- **Boundary scenario response:** Correctly handle 3 challenging interaction scenarios
- **Technical terminology:** Successfully transcribe 10 STEM-related terms

### Certification Requirements:
- **Attend complete 2-hour training session**
- **Pass competency assessment with 90%+ score**
- **Sign confidentiality agreement and volunteer commitment form**
- **Complete contact information and availability preferences**

## Post-Training Resources

### Reference Materials:
- **Quick reference card:** Pocket-sized reminder of key dos and don'ts
- **Technical terminology glossary:** Common terms by academic subject
- **Emergency contact information:** Programme coordinator and backup contacts
- **Feedback forms:** Links to online evaluation forms

### Ongoing Support:
- **Peer mentorship:** Pairing with experienced volunteers for first 3 examinations
- **Office hours:** Weekly drop-in time with programme coordinator
- **WhatsApp support group:** Peer network for questions and problem-solving
- **Advanced training opportunities:** Specialized workshops for complex subjects

## Customization Guidelines for Institutions

### Adapting for Different Contexts:
- **Small programmes (5-15 volunteers):** Combine sessions and extend practice time
- **Large programmes (30+ volunteers):** Split into multiple sessions with consistent content
- **Technical institutions:** Add 30 minutes for advanced STEM transcription training
- **Liberal arts colleges:** Include additional writing and essay transcription practice

### Language and Cultural Adaptations:
- **Regional language integration:** Add modules for local language transcription
- **Cultural sensitivity:** Include disability etiquette specific to local context
- **Religious considerations:** Address any cultural or religious accommodation needs
- **International students:** Add cross-cultural communication components

### Assessment Modifications:
- **Advanced volunteers:** Higher accuracy standards and leadership role preparation
- **Struggling volunteers:** Additional practice sessions and mentorship support
- **Subject specialists:** Specialized certification for technical subject areas
- **Returning volunteers:** Refresher training focusing on new policies and improvements
