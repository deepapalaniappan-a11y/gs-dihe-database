## Legal & Policy Context

India's Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act 2016 (RPwD Act) is one of the most comprehensive disability laws in the Global South. It recognises 21 categories of disability (expanded from the 7 categories under the earlier 1995 Act), mandates 5% reservation in higher education admissions for persons with benchmark disabilities, and requires all educational institutions to provide reasonable accommodation.

The UGC has issued specific directives on disability inclusion in higher education, including the Higher Education for Persons with Special Needs (HEPSN) scheme, mandatory establishment of Equal Opportunity Centres (EOCs) at universities, and guidelines on examination accommodations. The National Education Policy 2020 (NEP 2020) further emphasises inclusion, though implementation remains uneven.

Key judicial precedent: *Vikash Kumar v. UPSC* (2021) — the Supreme Court clarified that reasonable accommodation must be substantive, not merely formal, and must account for the specific nature of the disability.

NAAC accreditation criteria include disability-related metrics, creating institutional incentive for visible accessibility measures, though critics note this can produce performative compliance without substantive change.

Key patterns observed:

- IITs and IIMs have formal accommodation policies but implementation varies widely
- Private universities show extreme variation — from comprehensive support to near-total absence
- Equal Opportunity Centres exist at many institutions but staffing and functionality vary dramatically

## Distinctive Features

India's disability support landscape has several culturally distinctive features:

- **Scribe/volunteer culture**: A well-established system of providing scribes (amanuenses) for examinations, heavily reliant on student volunteers rather than paid professionals
- **WhatsApp-based support networks**: Informal peer support through WhatsApp groups is pervasive and often more responsive than formal institutional channels
- **Family involvement**: Families play a significantly larger role in securing and coordinating accommodations than in Western models
- **NGO partnerships**: Organisations like NCPEDP, Saksham, and Enable India supplement institutional capacity
